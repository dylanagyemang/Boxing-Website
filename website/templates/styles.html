Boxer-Puncher

A style of boxer who lacks the speed and mobility of a pure boxer and the strength and power of a slugger, however he blends the two styles to be able to compete strongly with either style. While it's possible for them to be outboxed by a superior boxer or overwhelmed by the power of a slugger he tends to do well against swarmers, whose natural kryptonite are generally sluggers. The power of a boxer puncher as well as his mobility allows him to potentially break down swarmers, can confuse and frustrate sluggers with their mobility, and overpower pure boxers with their power and ability to move fast enough to cut off the ring.
Popular boxer-punchers include: Anthony Joshua, Terrence Crawford* and Errol Spence.

Brawler

An aesthetically rough style of boxing, sometimes referred to as ‘dirty boxing’. Most notably used by the likes of Jack Johnson and Roberto Duran, brawlers tend to be aggressive and fight on the inside, using their superior strength to wrestle and punish their opponents in the clinch using hand traps sneaky uppercuts, savage body work, collar ties and over/underhooks to control their opponents and break their body and spirit. They tend to do well against swarmers because of their ability to smother and control their opponent they tend not to fair so well against pure boxers that can adjust or clinch well but also have the ability to dominate boxers if they can't adjust or maintain their rhythm.
Popular brawlers include: Roberto Duran, Derek Chisora, Henry Armstrong

Catch-and-Shoot

A crowd friendly style used by the likes of Dillian Whyte, where the boxer depends on his guard to block and catch punches and return fire before the opponent can return to guard. Used mostly by close range fighters who have little to no time to avoid or parry incoming blows.
Popular fighters that utilize this style include Evander Holyfield, Oscar Valdez, Antonio Margarito

Counter-Puncher

Controls the fight by timing his punches after, during, or right before his opponent throws a punch. Counter punchers tend to slow the fight pace down and pot shot their opponents. There are many different styles of Counter punchers and can be found in any and all stances and even different styles, some counter punchers prefer to fight on the outside waiting for their opponents to come in and others prefer to apply pressure and force their opponents to throw punches that they can then counter.
Popular counter punchers include: Floyd Mayweather, Saul 'Canelo' Alvarez, Juan Manuel Marquez

Pressure Fighter

Fights on the front foot, prefers to pin his opponent to the ropes and land at will.
Popular pressure fighters include: Joe Frazier, Jack Dempsey, Shawn Porter

Pure Boxer

The purest boxing style popularized by Muhammad Ali. The pure boxer depends on his footwork and accuracy to snipe and overwhelm his opponent with clean scoring punches. While they generally lack power; either due to depending on their mobility and hence, can’t properly “sit down” on their punches or they developed a pure boxing style to compensate for their lack of natural ability to hurt their opponents, they depend on frustrating their opponents and dragging them to the finish line, forcing their opponents to expend energy by missing power shots and chasing the boxer from post to post. Pure boxers are generally referred to as out-fighters however some pure boxers can fight on the 'inside' or 'outside'.
Popular boxers include: Floyd Mayweather, Larry Holmes and Ricardo Lopez.

Slugger

Another crowd friendly style, used notably by George Foreman. The slugger is a boxer with tremendous power, they generally lack hand speed or the ability to throw combinations. Resigned to throwing one or two shots at a time, they break down their opponents any time they land a single shot. You can never count a slugger out; they possess the power to knock out their opponent at any time. Stylistically their weakness tend to be pure boxers who outscore and tire out the slugger, sapping their power and avoiding their best punches.
Popular sluggers include: George Foreman, Deontay Wilder, Wisaksil Wangek

Swarmer

A crowd friendly style of boxing where the boxer closes the distance to overwhelm his opponent with combinations. Naturally the kryptonite of pure boxers because the storm of punches disrupts their rhythm and outscores their conservative pot shotting or counterpunching style. An adage by most swarmers is to take one punch to land two or three.
Popular swarmers include: Manny Pacquiao, Shawn Porter, Vasily Lomanchenko*

Switch-Hitter

A rare but dangerous boxing style, used notably by Terrence Crawford, Jaron Ennis and the late Marvelous Marvin Hagler. These boxers are dangerous in either the orthodox or Southpaw stance and generally possess power in both hands. They tend to be both ferocious and skilled in boxing on the outside or fighting on the inside, confusing and breaking down their opponents.
Popular switch-hitters include: 'Marvelous' Marvin Hagler, Terrence 'Bud' Crawford and Jaron 'Boots' Ennis
