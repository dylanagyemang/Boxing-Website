The Lead Uppercut - 5

A punch thrown with the lead hand at nearly a vertical angle toward the opponent’s chin. Invented by Samuel Elias aka 'Dutch Sam' in the late 18th century.
Notable Users: Ricardo Lopez, Gervonta 'Tank' Davis, 'Iron' Mike Tyson

The Rear Uppercut - 6

A punch thrown with the rear hand at a nearly vertical angle toward the opponent’s chin.
Notable Users: Jermall Charlo, Lennox Lewis, Riddick Bowe

Brace Uppercut

An uppercut thrown after picking up your opponent’s chin or framing them with your forearm or elbow or even a collar tie. Very useful for establishing control over your opponent and setting them up in close range.
Notable Users: Larry Holmes, Gervonta 'Tank' Davis, Jack Johnson

Inside/Close Uppercut

A variant of the uppercut where the arm remains very close to the body, used to lift an opponent's head at close range.
Notable Users: 'Iron' Mike Tyson, Henry Armstrong, Marcos Maidana

Long Range Uppercut

A variant of the uppercut where it’s angled up and outward to reach the opponent, used to counter the jab or reach a retreating opponent.
Notable Users: Manny Pacquiao, 'Sugar' Shane Mosley, Gennady Golovkin

Slide-Back Uppercut

An uppercut thrown while pulling away from the opponent, often used to counter aggressive oppenets
Notable Users: James Toney, Ricardo Lopez, Salvador Sanchez

Slip Uppercut

An uppercut thrown after slipping a punch, generally after slipping to the outside and countering beneath the opponent’s punch, usually a jab.
Notable Users: Mikey Garcia, Mark Magsayo, 'Prince' Naseem Hamed

Swiping Uppercut

The uppercut is thrown across the body, upon completing the upper cut it will be on the opposite side of the user, useful in the clinch, especially if the opponent's head is on your shoulder
Notable Users: Juan Manuel Marquez, Jarret Hurd, Miguel Cotto

Vertical Fist Uppercut

A variant of the uppercut where the palm doesn’t face the user but the opposite side of the user, useful for splitting the guard, and gaining head control if yoou miss
Notable Users: Terrence Crawford, David Benavidez,Andre Berto

Underhand Straight

Generally used by shorter fighters but can be used when in a crouched position, this punch is similar to the 45-degree jab where the punch comes up from a low angle.
Notable Users: Guillermo Rigondieux, Manny Pacquiao
